Breast Cancer Awareness

What is Breast Cancer?

Breast cancer is a disease where cells in the breast grow uncontrollably. It can occur in both men and women, though it is far more common in women. Early detection and treatment are key to increasing the chances of survival.

Symptoms of Breast Cancer

  • A lump or thickening in the breast or underarm.
  • Changes in the size, shape, or appearance of the breast.
  • Inverted nipple or nipple discharge.
  • Redness or flaky skin in the breast area.
  • Pain in the breast that doesn’t go away.

Prevention and Risk Factors

While some risk factors for breast cancer (like genetics) cannot be changed, others can be reduced. Common prevention strategies include:

  • Maintaining a healthy weight.
  • Staying physically active.
  • Limiting alcohol intake.
  • Breastfeeding, if possible.
  • Talking to your doctor about the risks of hormone replacement therapy.
